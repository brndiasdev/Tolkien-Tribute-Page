J. R. R. Tolkien

The creator of middle-earth

Here's a timeline of J. R. R. Tolkien's life:

  • 1892 - Born on January 3 in Bloemfontein, Orange Free State (now South Africa)
  • 1895 - Moves to England with his mother and younger brother after his father's death
  • 1900 - Begins studying at King Edward's School in Birmingham
  • 1904 - Experiences the death of his mother, leaving him and his brother orphaned
  • 1908 - Enters Exeter College, Oxford University
  • 1914 - Marries Edith Bratt
  • 1916 - Volunteers for military service in World War I and serves in France
  • 1917 - Publishes his first work, a poem called "The Battle of the Eastern Field"
  • 1925 - Becomes a professor at the University of Leeds
  • 1929 - Transfers to the University of Oxford as a professor of Anglo-Saxon
  • 1937 - Publishes "The Hobbit," a fantasy novel for children
  • 1939-1945 - World War II: Tolkien works as an external examiner for Oxford University and continues writing "The Lord of the Rings"
  • 1954-1955 - Publishes "The Lord of the Rings"
